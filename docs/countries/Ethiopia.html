Overview

Ethiopia operates one of the largest health systems in sub-Saharan Africa, organised in a three-tier structure: primary health care (health posts, health centres and primary hospitals), general hospitals, and specialised / tertiary hospitals. The Federal Ministry of Health maintains the centralised Master Facility Registry v2, and this dataset reflects a recent CSV export from that registry covering all 11 regional administrations and the two chartered city administrations.

Demographics

With a population of approximately 125 million — the second-largest in Africa — Ethiopia faces enormous service-delivery demand. The Health Extension Programme has placed two trained extension workers at each rural kebele-level health post, making the country a continental reference for community-based primary care. Doctor density remains low (~1.1 per 10,000), with nurses, midwives and Health Extension Workers carrying most of the front-line workload.

Services

MFR v2 records four service domains per facility — clinical, diagnostic, support, and 24-hour services — which the HealthScope ETL pipeline unnests and maps to the standard 3-level hierarchy used for every other country. The public MFR v2 CSV export does not include GPS, so coordinates are joined from the openly-licensed WHO AFRO compilation on the Humanitarian Data Exchange (~63% direct match by Facility ID); facilities without an HDX coordinate (mostly drug stores and small private clinics outside WHO AFRO’s traditional coverage scope) are excluded from the map.
