Overview

Botswana has one of the more developed health systems in sub-Saharan Africa, supported by the country’s relative economic prosperity from diamond revenues. The country has around 1,000 health facilities including clinics, primary hospitals, district hospitals, and referral hospitals. The government provides largely free primary health care to citizens, and the country has achieved remarkable success in its HIV response.

Demographics

With a population of approximately 2.5 million and a relatively low population density, Botswana’s health facilities serve both urban centres and widely dispersed rural communities. The country has a higher physician density than most African peers, at around 3–4 per 10,000 population. Mobile health facilities and outreach teams support populations in remote districts such as the Central Kalahari.

Services

Botswana’s facilities offer comprehensive primary care, one of Africa’s most successful HIV/ART programmes (with over 85% of eligible patients on treatment), TB services, maternal and child health, immunization, malaria diagnosis and treatment (primarily in the northern districts bordering Zimbabwe and Zambia), mental health services, and management of NCDs including diabetes and hypertension.

Service availability has been extracted from the Botswana Master Facility List and standardised through the HealthScope ETL pipeline into the same 3-level domain hierarchy used for every other country.
